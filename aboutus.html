Heinz, named after Henry J. Heinz, began as a small-scale food packing venture in Sharpsburg, Pennsylvania, in 1869, where Henry and a partner marketed horseradish. After facing bankruptcy, Henry, along with his brother and cousin, established F & J Heinz, producing the iconic Heinz Tomato Ketchup and other products. He later restructured it as the H. J. Heinz Company in 1888. The famous "57 varieties" slogan emerged in 1896, chosen by Henry almost randomly but for its appealing sound and psychological impact.
